General Description

Study Skills for Business 1 (SSB1) in the spring semester and SSB 2 in the fall semester will improve students’

English skills by focusing on developing writing, listening, note-taking, and speaking skills as well as vocabulary.

Students in the Faculty of Business Administration should take Study Skills for Business 1 (SSB1) in the spring

semester and SSB 2 in the fall semester together with Business English (BE) 1 in the spring semester and BE 2 in

the fall semester.

Goals and Objectives

This course follows the Curriculum for General Academic Skills

The aim is to help you study in English at the university and overseas

  • By the end of this course, for writing and speaking, you will be able to:
    • collect ideas, take notes, and research effectively (Invention)
    • organise your ideas clearly (Arrangement)
    • express your ideas clearly (Style)
    • present your ideas well (Presentation)
  • You will also work on:
    • vocabulary
    • reading and listening fluency
    • critical thinking
    • goal setting and time management
    • communicating in an academic environment
    • directing your own learning
    • using software and online tools effectively
    • using learning strategies
    • learning vocabulary and handling unknown words

Writing Class 2 (Mon 04/22)

What to prepare for this class:

  • do a quickwrite, learn vocabulary, do a speed reading
  • Download the templates
    • TEMPLATE for ESSAY OUTLINE
    • TEMPLATE for ESSAY
  • CHOOSE A TOPIC! Read the instructions on good topics.
  • Read the project page
    • Spring: write an essay about ethical consumption
    • Fall: write an essay about endangered animals
  • Read the example essay (see project page for link)
  • Research your topic A LITTLE:
    • Check you have 3 problems and 3 solutions
    • Enough to give a 3-5 minute presentation next week
      • What is your topic? How is it made? Where from? Etc.
      • What problems does it cause?
      • What solutions are there?
      • Are the solutions working?
    • Read Wikipedia, but remember you CANNOT use your notes in the essay
    • Read at least 3 sources about your topic and take notes
    • Read JAPANESE sources, but take notes in English
    • Take handwritten notes for now.
    • I will explain the typed template next week.
    • Keywords / Note english only - this is an outline
    • Don't copy-paste!
    • Keep a note of where you found your sources (the links).
    • Don't worry about APA for now.

What we will do in class:

  • Share your research in pairs
  • Check topics are OK
  • Check outlines
  • Review APA
  • How to use “Template for Essay Outline”
